OPERATOR GUIDE

EdgeWalker Reference

What EdgeWalker Does

EdgeWalker watches SOXL, classifies the market as uptrend, sideways, or downtrend, and routes the decision to a specialist bot. It can enter, manage a trailing exit, close stale exposures, and stop taking new entries near the closeout window.

Bot Roles

MomentumBot buys SOXL when the trend is up. ChopBot buys SOXL only when a sideways market offers enough discount to the slow SMA. InverseBot buys SOXS when SOXL is in a downtrend.

Key Controls

Directional mode controls how selective trend entries are. Position sizing chooses fixed dollars or a percent of buying power, including a custom percent when the presets are too coarse. Trail percent controls the protective trailing exit. Regime gap and regime exit tune trend routing and hysteresis. Chop discount controls how deep a sideways pullback must be.

Status And Metrics

Regime, Active bot, Routed, and Action explain the current decision path. Broker state and Order Flow show execution constraints. Session P/L, Position P/L, and Bot Performance separate account movement from strategy behavior.

Narrative And Analysis

Narrative turns selected logs into a readable debrief. Analysis is advisory: it can suggest what to watch or test next, but it should not be treated as automatic tuning or a profitability claim.

Safety Notes

EdgeWalker is an operator tool, not financial advice. Leveraged ETFs can move quickly, paper results do not guarantee live outcomes, and the operator remains responsible for configuration, supervision, and trading risk.
